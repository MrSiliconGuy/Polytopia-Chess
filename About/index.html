Polytopia Chess

Polytopia is a fun, turn-based 4X game created by Midjiwan. Check out Polytopia here.

Polytopia Chess uses the battle mechanics of Polytopia to create a chess-like game. Instead of a randomly generated terrain, Polytopia Chess pits units to a strategic battle on the familiar 8x8 chessboard.
With your army of Xinxi, Imperius or Bardur units, defeat your opponents through tactical battle strategy

This game can be played local multiplayer on a single computer, or even played multiplayer online

Creating a Board Setup

Setup a board using the board setup creator

Left click to place a unit, and right click to remove a unit.
You can choose the type of unit, as well as the tribe of the unit using the drop-down menus at the bottom. Different units have different abilities, strengths and weaknesses, just like in the original game
You can clear your current board setup using the button at the bottom right. You can also load an example setup with the bottom on the left if you are too lazy to make your own ;)

The default setup has two tribes, Xinxi and Imperius. You can change the number of tribes, as well as the tribes order by clicking on the 'Edit' button

The board setup is auto-saved to the browser (as long as cookies are enabled). You can close and reopen your tab at any time.

Exporting Game

To export a board setup for later use or to start a multiplayer game, click the Export button at the bottom. Copy the resulting code from the text box. This is the code representing your board setup, which you can use to start singleplayer or multiplayer games.

How to Play

(This guide assumes you know the basic battle mechanics of Polytopia, including health, unit movement, attacking other units, and basic knowledge of what the different units are. Further information about individual unit stats can be found here and here)

Similar to Polytopia, Polytopia Chess is turn-based. You play by moving your units one by one, then ending your turn.
A game starts with the player of the first tribe in the turn order (by default this is Xinxi). Select units by clicking on them, and move, attack enemy units, or perform other actions as desired.

On your turn, you may perform the following actions in any order:

  • Move a unit
    • Move a unit by clicking on it and then clicking on a square that has turned blue

  • Attack an enemy unit
    • Attack an enemy unit by clicking on it and then clicking on an enemy unit that has turned red

  • Recover health [+2 health]
    • Recover a unit's health by clicking on it and then clicking on the 'recover' button at the bottom.
      Only available if the unit hasn't moved or attacked yet, and has less than maximum health.

  • Heal friendly units/Convert enemy units [Mindbender only]
    • Heal friendly units by clicking on the mindbender and then clicking on the 'heal' button. Adjacent friendly units will recover +4 health.
      Only available if the mindbender unit is adjacent to friendly units with less than maximum health
    • Convert enemy units in the same manner as attacking them.

  • Disband a unit
    • Disband a unit by clicking on it and then clicking on the 'disband' button at the bottom. (P.S. This is rarely useful)

  • Upgrade to Veteran [available after 3 kills]
    • Upgrade a unit to veteran status by clicking on it and then clicking on the 'veteran' button at the bottom.
      That unit will recover to full health plus 5 bonus health.

Turn limitations: On your turn, your units have a limited number of moves and attacks.
In total, you have a maximum of 2 moves, 2 attacks, plus an additional action of either moving or attacking.
(That is, you may either move twice and attack three times, or attack twice or move three times)
Once you have reached this limit, you will no longer be able to move or attack with any units

Other actions are not limited by this. i.e. You may heal and recover as many times as you'd like.

To finish your turn, you may either click the 'end turn' button in the bottom right corner, or press [Enter].
An end turn confirmation dialogue will pop up. Click OK to end your turn and pass play to the next player.

Local Multiplayer

To play Polytopia Chess on a single computer ("singleplayer"), click here

You must first create a board setup to play a local game. Click 'Make Board Setup' at the bottom and follow the above instructions for setting up a board.

After setting up a board, you may click 'Save & Return to game' to begin playing.
Alternatively, you may paste a game code by clicking 'Import' to start a local game.

Singleplayer games are auto-saved to the browser. You may close and reopen your tab at any time.

Online Multiplayer

To play Polytopia Chess with others online ("multiplayer"), click here

To create a game, click 'Create Game' and paste a board setup code. You will be given an 8 digit invite code. Use this to invite your friends to join your game.

To join a game, click 'Join Game' and input the 8 digit invite code. Once everyone has joined, you may begin playing.
The order of play is the same as the order that the players joined the game, i.e. the host is always player 1, the second to join is always player 2, etc.
